Lesson 2: Formatting Cells & Ranges

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Apply formatting accurately to cells and ranges
  • Distinguish between formatting individual cells and entire ranges
  • Use formatting to clarify meaning and improve readability
  • Recognize how poor formatting interferes with analysis
  • Prepare data ranges for formulas, tables, and charts

Why Cell & Range Formatting Matters

At this point in the course, Excel work shifts from organizing data to working with data.

Formulas, tables, and charts all depend on predictable, consistent ranges.

If formatting is inconsistent or misleading:

  • Calculations may fail
  • Filters and sorts may behave unexpectedly
  • Charts may misrepresent data
  • Other users may misinterpret results

Key idea:

Formatting communicates intent to both Excel and people.
